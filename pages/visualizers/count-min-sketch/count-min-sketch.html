📊 Count-Min Sketch Explained

➕

Update: d Hash Functions

To insert item X, compute d independent hash functions h₁(X), h₂(X), …, h_d(X). Each gives a column index in its row. Increment those d cells by 1. That's it — X is never stored; only counters change.

🔍

Query: Take the Minimum

To estimate the frequency of X, read the d cells — one per row at column h_i(X). Return the minimum. Any cell may be inflated by collisions with other items, but the minimum gives the tightest upper bound.

📐

One-Directional Error

The sketch can overestimate but never underestimate. With width w and depth d, the probability that any estimate exceeds the true count by more than ε·N (total items) is at most (1/e)^d — controlled by depth alone.

🏆

Heavy Hitters & Zipf's Law

Real-world frequencies follow Zipf's Law — rank-1 items appear far more than rank-2, etc. A Count-Min Sketch paired with a min-heap identifies the top-k most frequent items without storing every distinct element — the technique behind Twitter trending and Google hot-searches.
