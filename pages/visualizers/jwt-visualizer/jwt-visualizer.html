Anatomy of a JWT

1. Header

Typically consists of two parts: the type of the token (JWT) and the signing algorithm being used, such as HMAC SHA256 or RSA. It is Base64Url encoded to form the first part of the token.

2. Payload

Contains the claims. Claims are statements about an entity (typically, the user) and additional data. It is Base64Url encoded to form the second part of the token. Do not put secrets here, as anyone can decode it.

3. Signature

Created by taking the encoded header, the encoded payload, a secret, and the algorithm specified in the header, and signing that. This verifies that the sender is who it says it is and ensures the message wasn't changed along the way.
